# Case studies

Program retrospectives. Unlike postmortems (which focus on a single incident), case studies tell the arc of a program: what it was, how it ran, what worked, what I'd do differently.

## Index

- [Finest Minds · $1.2M · 5 teams · 92% on-time · anonymized](./finest-minds-program-retrospective.md)

## Writing rules

1. **Name the outcome in the first paragraph.** If the reader stops there, they know whether to keep going.
2. **Anonymize what must be anonymized. Keep structure intact.** Redacted doesn't mean vague — the mechanics should be legible.
3. **"What I'd do differently" is the most valuable section.** The one where the author admits mistakes or second-guesses decisions is the section that earns the reader's trust.
